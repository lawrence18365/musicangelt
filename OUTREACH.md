# Venue Partnership Outreach — MusicAngel

A queue of personalised partnership emails to wedding events teams at the 36 venues with dedicated MusicAngel pages. **Do not send in bulk.** Send 5/day max — wedding venue events teams are small and respond to genuine outreach, not mass mailing.

## The setup before you send

1. **Find the right human at each venue.** Look for "Wedding Coordinator," "Events Manager," or "Sales Manager" on LinkedIn or the venue's events page. First-name personalisation is non-negotiable.
2. **Email address pattern.** Most Irish venue events teams use `events@venuename.ie` or `weddings@venuename.com`. Use the venue's own contact form if email is gated.
3. **Send time.** Tuesday–Thursday, 9-11am IST. Avoid Mondays (inbox flood) and Fridays (mental checkout).
4. **Subject line goal.** A wedding coordinator opens 100+ emails a day. The subject must read as "this is a real human about a real wedding" — not as outreach.
5. **Follow up once.** 5-7 days after the first email. Then move on.
6. **Track in a spreadsheet.** Date sent, response, next step.

## Realistic expectations

- **Open rate:** 60-80% (these are inboxes that respond to real wedding business)
- **Reply rate:** 15-25% (some will say yes immediately, more will say "we'll add you to the list")
- **Listing rate:** 5-10% of total sent (so expect 2-3 actual recommended-supplier adds from 30)
- **Booking attribution within 12 months:** 1-3 weddings traceable to outreach in year one

The compounding value is the SEO link equity from venues' suppliers pages — that builds over years.

## What NOT to do

- Don't bulk-send via a tool (Mailchimp / Apollo). These inboxes filter or report bulk mail.
- Don't include attachments or oversized images.
- Don't follow up more than once. After follow-up #1 with no reply, move on.
- Don't write "I hope this email finds you well." Use a real opener.
- Don't include video or "look at my showreel!" — let the link do the work.
