Drinks reception music. the hour that sets the tone

The drinks reception is the first 60-90 minutes guests spend at your wedding. What's playing while they're holding a glass of fizz sets the entire mood of the day.

Most couples overthink the evening band setlist and underthink drinks reception music. It's the opposite of where the attention should be. The evening band has guests as a captive dance-floor audience. The drinks reception is when guests are forming first impressions, and what they hear shapes how they remember the day.

What works: the rules

  • Acoustic and vocal-led. A full PA-driven band feels too loud while people are still saying hello. Strip it back.
  • Mid-tempo, never high-tempo. You're not at the dance floor stage. Songs that lean conversational, not danceable.
  • Recognisable melodies, light arrangements. Familiar enough to ease conversation, not loud enough to interrupt it.
  • 60-90 minutes of material. Plus a quiet outro for when the call to dinner happens.
  • Live, ideally. Spotify works in a pinch but a live musician in the corner with a guitar or piano elevates the whole hour.
"The drinks reception is the only hour of the day where your guests are quiet enough to actually hear the music. Don't waste it on a Spotify playlist."

  6. Make You Feel My Love · Bob Dylan / Adele

  8. Sea of Love · Cat Power

  10. Falling Slowly · Glen Hansard & Markéta Irglová
  11. Stand By Me · Ben E. King

  14. Best Part · Daniel Caesar & H.E.R.
  15. First Day of My Life · Bright Eyes
  16. Ho Hey · The Lumineers

What to avoid

  • Up-tempo dance-floor songs, because they signal "go dance" when guests should be talking.
  • Anything with lyrics about heartbreak, exes, or break-ups.
  • Loud full-band PA versions of any song.
  • Awkward silences, because a 90-min playlist with 60 mins of songs leaves 30 mins of dead air.
  • Spotify autoplay leading into completely off-tone tracks once your curated playlist runs out.

Frequently asked questions

What music suits a wedding drinks reception?

Acoustic, mid-tempo, vocal-led music with a relaxed feel, typically a singer-guitarist duo or trio, or an acoustic version of the evening band's lineup. The mood is warm and conversational, not full-band party energy yet.

How long is the drinks reception music slot?

Typically 60-90 minutes, depending on how the wedding flow runs. Most Irish weddings have a single acoustic set covering arrivals and drinks before guests are called to dinner.

Do MusicAngel bands cover drinks reception?

Yes. Acoustic drinks reception sets are a standard paid add-on to any evening band package, typically a stripped-down version of the band, vocalist and guitarist or acoustic trio.

Should the drinks reception music match the evening band?

It helps with continuity if the same band covers both, guests recognise the voices when the evening set begins. Some couples book separate acts (harpist, string trio) for a different atmosphere.
